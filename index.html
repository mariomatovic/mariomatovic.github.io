What is Biopsychology?

Biopsychology, also called behavioral neuroscience, is the branch of psychology that studies how our brain and nervous system influence our thoughts, emotions, and behaviors. It explores how biological processes—like brain activity, hormones, and genetics—interact with our experiences to shape how we think, feel, and act. In simple terms, it’s the science of understanding the mind through the body.

This principle is the foundation of my work. Whether with an individual athlete or a corporate team, my goal is not to teach, but to facilitate growth. Some of the psychological concepts used

  • Team dynamics — social cognition, communication patterns, team cohesion, leadership, team identity, standards, and values.
  • Thought patterns — automatic thoughts, cognitive distortions, schemas, core beliefs, and defense mechanisms.
  • Emotional regulation — window of tolerance, emotional clarity, suppression, distraction, acceptance, and expression.
  • Core beliefs and identity — self-schemas, implicit self-schemas, narrative identity, and internalized scripts.
  • Motivation — self-determination theory, self-efficacy, locus of control, intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation.
  • Meaning and purpose — coherence, purpose, significance, values clarification, cognitive reframing, narrative reconstruction.
  • Resilience and adaptation — psychological flexibility, coping repertoires, neuroplasticity, and recovery.
  • Integration and wholeness — self-integration, congruence, mind–body coherence, holistic processing, and authentic functioning.
  • Attention, concentration, and flow — flow state, selective attention, working memory under stress, and mindfulness.
